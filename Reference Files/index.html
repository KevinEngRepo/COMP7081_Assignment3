  1. Introduction

    2. The project proposal is for a system to allow the rental and return of vehicles, exchanging of accrued loyalty points to rent or buy vehicles, and to allow the customer to buy insurance. Our client is the local car rental company, SuperRent.

    Developer information:

    David: Network Security, User Interface, Software Developer

    Ashu: Software Developer, Systems Analyst, Business Analyst

    Lili: student from CST bachelor program with extended knowledge about database.

    Company information:

    The main operation for SuperRent is to rent out cars and trucks to its customers. The company has multiple locations in several cities. In order to meet customers' differing requirements, the company offers rentals on a broad range of cars and trucks. The reward system also involves different discounts for the company's loyal customers.

    Project information:

    The project includes four components:

    • Customer membership, points system, reservations
    • Customer vehicle rental, return, point deduction, and insurance purchase
    • Assistance from clerk for customer vehicle rentals, return
    • Manager association for used vehicle sale, sales rates, cost, reports

    The component included in this project is the second part: customer vehicle rental, return, point deduction, and insurance purchase.

  2. Background

    3. Project description:

    This project is one of the components for the whole SuperRent system. The user is the customer. In this component, our software system mainly includes four activities: customer vehicle rental, return, point deduction, and insurance purchase:

    • Customer vehicle rental: after the customers provide their information, they can make a reservation and have the rental agreement with credit card payment information.
    • For the vehicle return, the customer need to provide their data, time, odometer reading and make sure the gas tank is full.
    • The reward system includes the rules about how to collect points and deduct points. When a customer joins the club, he or she receives 500 points. After the initial points, club members gain 1 point for every $5 they spend. To deduct the points, the club members can exchange 1,000 points for a one day rental of a premium or lower-ranking car. Alternatively, 1,500 points for a one day renal for a luxury car, SUV, van or truck.
    • The customer can decide whether they need to have insurance purchase for the whole rental period. The payment need to be made at the point that vehicle is returned.

    This component in our project will help the company collect the information about the vehicle rental, payment, point tracking, return and insurance purchase.

  5. Process Model

    6. The requirements that we need to fill for the SuperRent system are:

    1. Can rent a vehicle
    2. Can return a rented vehicle
    3. Exchange points to rent a vehicle
    4. Exchange points to buy a vehicle
    5. Buy insurance for a vehicle

    There are many different kinds of process models that we can could apply to this project but the one that we as a group feel best fit for this product is to do it in an agile process. We will need to divide up the functionality and arrange it in a manner that would allow us to get feedback quickly and effectively from the client. The whole behind us using the agile process model is to deliver a product to the customer that will be useful and allow them to be able to track the progress of the overall solution. With terms of Agile the client and we will be using the Scrum framework to manage the project.

    Once the requirements are done with the client we will work with them to identify the number of sprints we will need and what requirements will be worked on each sprint. We plan on having sprints at least a week or max two weeks long and at the end of each sprint we will do a deployment to a testing environment so the client can look at it and play around with it if they feel the need to do so. This will also allow us to show the client the progress of the project and in case they are not happy about a certain requirement or they change their mind it will be easy to change the functionality at that point.

    After each sprint there will be a new build that goes out on the testing environment. Depending on the requirements the one week sprints are not finalized as some pieces of functionality will need to take longer to build. After the sprint is done we will spend at least one week testing the build to make sure all the components work are implemented according to the requirements and to the needs of the client.

    This process will give the clients the biggest return on their investment because they are able to track the progress of the project and easier to track changes as well as we can easily adapt any changes.
